# Nakha — Pre-Launch "Teaser & Hype" Plan
**Market:** Bechar, Algeria
**Window:** ~3 weeks pre-launch (1 week soft teasers → 1 week story building → 7-day countdown)
**Primary goal:** Build a waitlist of 1,000+ qualified locals (customers) and recruit the first 15 founding cooks before the platform opens.
**Tone:** Warm, authentic, "ابن الحي" (the guy from your neighborhood) — friendly فصحى with a Bechari soul.

---

## 1. Campaign Overview

**Campaign name (suggested):** *"بيتنا فيه طبّاخ"* — "Our home has a cook"
A line that triggers nostalgia for grandma's kitchen, frames Nakha as a return to home cooking, and invites people to wonder *"who is the cook in MY neighborhood?"*

**One-sentence summary:**
A 3-week social-first hype campaign in Bechar that turns local home cooks into local heroes, builds a waitlist of curious customers, and makes "Early Access" feel like an invitation to a private dinner — not a tech launch.

**Primary KPI:** 1,000 waitlist signups (phone number captured) + 15 founding cooks confirmed before launch day.

**Secondary KPIs:**
- 50,000+ video views across Instagram Reels + TikTok
- 5+ local micro-influencers activated
- 30% waitlist → first-order conversion in week 1 post-launch

## 2. Target Audience

### Primary: The Hungry Bachelor / Working Professional (الزبون)
- Age 22–40, lives in Bechar (مركز, العقيد لطفي, بشار الجديدة, debdaba)
- Single, student, or working away from family — eats out 4+ times a week
- Tired of pizza, fast food, and identical restaurant menus
- Misses "أكلة البيت" — home cooking, especially traditional Bechari dishes (chakhchoukha, mfatech, taguella, rechta)
- Pain point: "ما كاينش وين تاكل أكلة بيت كي راك بعيد على دارك"

**Where they are:** Instagram, TikTok, Facebook groups (*"بشار للجميع"*, *"بشار اليوم"*), university WhatsApp groups, the corniche cafés in the evening.

### Primary: The Home Cook (الطبّاخة)
- Women 30–60, often housewives or supplementing income
- Already cooks daily for family — has the skill, lacks the platform
- May already sell informally to neighbors via WhatsApp
- Pain point: "نطيب مليح بصح ما عندي وين نبيع بطريقة محترمة"

**Where they are:** Facebook groups for women, family WhatsApp networks, mosque circles, neighborhood word-of-mouth.

### Secondary: The Family Buying for Guests
- Couples 30–50 with kids, hosting unexpected guests, or too tired to cook on a Friday
- Want quality + tradition, not fast food

## 3. Core Message & Tone

**Core message:**
> "نَكهة — أكلة بيت، من جارك، توصلك."
> *(Nakha — home cooking, from your neighbor, delivered to you.)*

**Three supporting messages:**
1. **Authenticity:** "ماشي مطعم، دار حقيقية، طبّاخة حقيقية." (Not a restaurant. A real home, a real cook.)
2. **Community:** "كل طلب يدعم عائلة من بشار." (Every order supports a family from Bechar.)
3. **Trust:** "نحن نختار الطبّاخات بأنفسنا." (We choose every cook ourselves.)

**Tone notes (CLAUDE.md compliance):**
The project rule is فصحى for in-app UI. For social media teasers, write in **warm فصحى مبسّطة** that *sounds* like a brother talking — short sentences, direct address ("يا أخي", "يا أختي", "تخيّل معي"), questions, not corporate phrasing. The cook in videos can speak دارجة بشارية naturally — that's part of the authenticity. Captions stay فصحى.

## 4. Teaser Content Ideas (Reels / TikTok)

The rule for the first 14 days: **show the food, never show the app.** Build mystery.

### Concept 1 — "يدّ تطيب" (Hands That Cook)
Close-up shots only of hands: kneading dough, stirring a pot of marqa, folding bourek, pouring tea. No face. No logo. Caption:
> "في بشار، فيه يدّ تطيب لك أحسن من أمك. قريب تعرفها."
> *(In Bechar, there's a hand that cooks for you better than your mom. You'll meet her soon.)*

Length: 15s. Music: traditional Algerian instrumental (oud/gasba), no copyrighted track.

### Concept 2 — "البخار" (The Steam)
A single cinematic shot of steam rising from a covered tagine, rolling onto a wooden table at sunset. No words for 10s. Then text appears:
> "شي قريب يفوح في بشار."
> *(Something is about to fill Bechar with its scent.)*
> — @nakha.dz

### Concept 3 — "البحث المستحيل" (The Impossible Search)
POV-style Reel: a tired guy after work scrolling through pizza/burger delivery apps, sighing, closing the phone. Cut to black. Text:
> "وين راهي أكلة البيت؟"
> *(Where did home cooking go?)*

End card: "نَكهة — قريباً" + waitlist link.

### Concept 4 — "صوت الحارة" (Sounds of the Neighborhood)
ASMR-style: the sound of onions sizzling, bread breaking, a kettle whistling, a knife on a cutting board. No visuals beyond extreme close-ups. Caption:
> "إذا هذي الأصوات تفكّرك في دار جدّتك… انت مدعو."
> *(If these sounds remind you of your grandmother's house… you're invited.)*

### Concept 5 — "الطبّاخة المجهولة" (The Unknown Cook)
A silhouette of a woman cooking, filmed from behind in low light, traditional music playing. Voiceover (her, in دارجة):
> "أنا نطيب من 30 سنة. قريب، تذوقوا."
> *(I've been cooking for 30 years. Soon, you'll taste it.)*

This becomes a recurring series: "الطبّاخة 1", "الطبّاخة 2"… each week reveals a new silhouette.

### Concept 6 — "التحدي" (The Challenge)
Stop-motion of a chakhchoukha being assembled in 10 seconds. Text:
> "تقدر تطيبها أحسن؟ ولا تفضل تطلبها؟"
> *(Can you cook it better? Or would you rather order it?)*
> [waitlist link]

### Posting cadence (weeks 1–2)
- **3 Reels/TikToks per week** (Mon, Wed, Fri evenings — 8 PM Bechar time)
- **Daily story** showing behind-the-scenes hand-only clips
- **Zero feed posts** in week 1 (only Reels + Stories — keeps mystery)

## 5. Scarcity & Exclusivity Tactics

The frame: **Nakha is a private supper club, not a delivery app.** People should feel they were *invited*, not *signed up*.

### Tactic A — "أول 100 ضيف" (The First 100 Guests)
- The first 100 people on the waitlist get **lifetime free delivery** + a personal welcome message from the founder
- Display a **live counter** on the landing page: "متبقّى 47 مقعد من أول 100"
- Promote the counter weekly on stories ("بقات 12 فرصة فقط")

### Tactic B — Invite-Only Codes (the real magic)
- Each waitlist signup receives **3 personal invite codes** to share with friends
- Friends who join via your code → both of you move up the waitlist
- This turns every signup into a free distribution channel
- Codes look human: `NKH-AHMED-23` instead of `XK7P9Q`

### Tactic C — "قائمة الجيران" (The Neighbor List)
- During signup, ask which حي (neighborhood) the customer lives in
- Show a meter per neighborhood: "حي العقيد لطفي: 67 جار مسجّل، ينقص 33 باش نفتحوا أول طبّاخة فيه"
- Creates **neighborhood-level FOMO** — people pressure neighbors to sign up so their area "unlocks"

### Tactic D — Founding Cooks Badge (already in your model)
Lean into the 15-cook founding member status publicly:
- A "بطاقة الطبّاخ المؤسس" (founding cook card) — a beautiful Arabic certificate, physically printed and delivered to each of the first 15
- Photo of each receiving her badge → posted on the page → social proof + scarcity ("بقات 4 أماكن فقط")

### Tactic E — The Closed Telegram Channel
- Anyone on the waitlist gets access to a private Telegram channel: *"نادي نَكهة"*
- Inside: behind-the-scenes content, polls, sneak peeks of menus, first dibs on launch
- Being "inside" feels like belonging to a club, not a mailing list

## 6. Engagement Hooks (Community Activation)

These get people to *participate*, not just consume — which is what drives waitlist signups.

### Hook 1 — "خمّن الطبّاخة" (Guess the Cook)
Weekly Instagram/TikTok game:
- Post a photo of a finished dish + 3 cryptic clues about the cook ("تسكن في حي قديم، عندها 4 أولاد، تطيب الرشتة كل جمعة")
- People guess in comments
- Winner (correct + most creative comment) gets a **free meal on launch day**
- The actual cook's reveal video is then posted = double engagement

### Hook 2 — "صوّت لطبق الافتتاح" (Vote for the Launch Dish)
- Post 4 traditional Bechari dishes (chakhchoukha, mfatech, taguella, rechta)
- Run a 48-hour story poll
- Winning dish becomes the **official "Day 1 Dish"** — every founding cook prepares it on launch day
- Voters who picked the winning dish get **20% off their first order**

### Hook 3 — "حكاية أكلة" (A Dish's Story)
- Invite people to share in comments: "أحسن أكلة بيت كليتها في عمرك — منين كانت؟"
- Best 5 stories featured on the page (with permission), each gets a free meal
- Generates emotional content + UGC + email/phone collection

### Hook 4 — "ابعت طبّاخة" (Nominate a Cook)
- Invite the community to nominate a cook they know: a mom, an aunt, a neighbor
- Each nomination = entry to a draw for a free family meal
- Doubles as **lead generation for the supply side** — solves the cold-start cook recruitment

### Hook 5 — The Bechar Cookbook Project
- Crowdsource one traditional recipe per week from the community
- Best recipe each week is cooked by a Nakha cook, filmed, and posted
- Gives ownership of the brand to the community before launch

### Hook 6 — "صورة الجار" (Neighbor Photo)
- Once you have 50 waitlist signups in a neighborhood, organize a small free *دفعة شاي* (tea gathering) at a local café
- Meet 10–20 of the most active waitlisters in person
- Cost: ~2,000 DZD per gathering. Returns: loyalty, word-of-mouth, video content for socials

## 7. Micro-Influencer Strategy

In Bechar, **trust is local**. A nano-influencer with 5,000 engaged Bechari followers beats a national star with 500,000.

### Tier 1 — Local Foodies (target: 5)
Look for accounts that already review Bechar restaurants or post local food content. Names will be real Bechar food pages, e.g., عشاق الأكل البشاري or similar.
**Offer:** Free tasting from 3 founding cooks before launch + exclusive first-week badge on their profile + a small payment (5,000–10,000 DZD per partner).
**Ask:** 1 Reel + 3 stories + 1 feed post.

### Tier 2 — Community Figures (target: 3)
Imam-adjacent voices, popular teachers, local poets, beloved café owners — people who don't post much but are *trusted*. Offer them a private dinner from a founding cook + a personal video thank-you. They post a sincere recommendation, not an ad.

### Tier 3 — University Influencers (target: 2)
A popular student at Université Tahri Mohamed Béchar. They reach the bachelor-customer segment. Offer: a meal-credit budget to give to friends + a per-signup commission (50 DZD per qualified signup using their code) for the first month post-launch.

### Tier 4 — Mom Influencers (target: 2)
Bechari women who post family/cooking content. Critical for **recruiting cooks**. Offer them the founding cook badge themselves if they want it, or a finder's fee per cook they bring in (3,000 DZD per approved cook).

### Influencer Brief (give this to every partner)
- ✅ Talk about the *feeling* of home cooking, not the app's features
- ✅ Show real food, real people, your real reaction
- ✅ Mention the invite-code system and waitlist
- ❌ Don't say "تطبيق" (application) — say "خدمة" (service) or "فكرة جديدة" (new idea)
- ❌ Don't post a polished ad — post like you're telling a friend about something cool

### Influencer Activation Calendar
| Week | Tier 1 (Foodies) | Tier 2 (Community) | Tier 3 (Students) | Tier 4 (Moms) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 (teaser) | Tease only ("شي جاي قريب…") | Silent | Silent | Recruit cooks |
| Week 2 (story building) | Reveal: tasted the food | First mention | Tease | Recruit + tease |
| Week 3 (countdown) | Daily content | Launch endorsement | Daily push | Daily content |

## 8. The 7-Day Countdown Campaign

The final week. Volume, energy, urgency. One theme per day.

### Day 7 — "العدّ تنازلي يبدأ" (The Countdown Begins)
- **Reveal post:** the logo, the name, the date. Big.
- Caption: "بعد 7 أيام، بشار تتغيّر طريقة أكلها. قائمة الانتظار: [link]"
- All influencers post the same hour (8 PM)
- WhatsApp broadcast to existing waitlist: "أنت من الأوائل. شارك الكود مع 3 أصدقاء."

### Day 6 — "تعرّفوا على الطبّاخات" (Meet the Cooks)
- Reveal the first 5 founding cooks (full faces now, with their permission)
- Mini-portrait videos: 30 seconds each, "أنا فاطمة، نطيب من 25 سنة، أحسن طبق عندي…"
- Drives emotional connection + signups

### Day 5 — "تعرّفوا على الأطباق" (Meet the Dishes)
- Carousel/Reel showcasing the actual menu of opening dishes
- Prices revealed (anchors expectations)
- Story: "أيّ طبق راح تطلب أوّل؟" (poll)

### Day 4 — "كيف يخدم" (How It Works)
- 60-second explainer Reel (animated or screen recording — first time the app is shown)
- 3 steps: اختار، اطلب، استنّى الطبّاخة
- Address top concerns: nutrition, hygiene, delivery time

### Day 3 — "شهادات" (Testimonials)
- The micro-influencers who tasted in week 2 post their reviews now
- UGC compilation Reel
- Pin top reviews to the profile

### Day 2 — "آخر فرصة للأوائل" (Last Chance to Be Among the First)
- Push the "first 100 free delivery" tactic hard
- Counter on landing page now reads "بقات X مقعد فقط"
- Story countdown sticker enabled

### Day 1 — "ليلة الافتتاح" (Launch Eve)
- Live session on Instagram with the founder + 2 founding cooks
- Talk about the journey, answer questions, build personal connection
- Final push: "غدا الساعة 10 صباحاً، نَكهة تفتح أبوابها."
- Email + SMS the entire waitlist that night with personal message

### Day 0 — Launch Day
- 7 AM: stories from cooks preparing
- 10 AM: site goes live
- 12 PM: first-order announcements ("الطلب الأول من حي العقيد لطفي ✅")
- All day: live updates of orders being delivered
- Evening: thank-you post + first-day numbers shared transparently

## 11. Waitlist Mechanics (the technical bit)

You'll need a simple landing page (can be a single Vite route on `nakha-topaz.vercel.app/waitlist` before main launch):

**Form fields:**
- Name (الاسم)
- Phone (رقم الهاتف) — primary key, used for SMS later
- Neighborhood (الحي) — required, drives the "neighbor list" tactic
- Optional: "هل تعرف طبّاخة؟" (do you know a cook?) checkbox → routes to cook recruitment

**Storage:**
- New Firestore collection: `waitlist/`
- Schema: `{ name, phone, neighborhood, inviteCode, invitedBy, position, createdAt }`
- Auto-generate invite codes on signup (e.g., `NKH-{first 5 chars of name}-{random 2 digits}`)

**Post-signup flow:**
1. Show the user their position ("أنت رقم 147 في القائمة")
2. Show their 3 invite codes
3. Show their neighborhood's progress bar
4. Auto-add to Telegram channel (link)

This data becomes your **launch-day SMS list** and your founding-customer pool.

## 14. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Low signups in week 1 (mystery is *too* mysterious) | Have a "soft reveal" Reel ready by day 5 of week 1 if signups <100 |
| Cooks drop out before launch | Recruit 20 to land 15. Start cook outreach in parallel with day 1 |
| Single-neighborhood concentration | Show neighborhood meter publicly to pressure under-represented areas |
| Influencer post falls flat | Brief them properly + give them tasting first so endorsement is real |
| Launch-day demand exceeds supply | Limit to 3 orders per cook per day, communicate it as "exclusive" |
